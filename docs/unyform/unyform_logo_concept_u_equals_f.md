# Unyform logo concept: **U as the “=” inside the F**

This concept leans into what Unyform *does* for enterprises: it makes AI output **conform** to standards (policy, style, security, context) — i.e., it makes things **equal** to the bar you set. The “=” becomes the visual shorthand for **uniformity / consistency / governance**, and it’s built from (or reveals) the **U** in *Unyform*.

Reference context: “Enterprise AI Trust and Consistency Layer” + the Ingest / Learn / Generate / Govern framing. fileciteturn0file0 fileciteturn0file1

---

## Core idea (in one sentence)

**Take the U, rotate / abstract it into two parallel strokes ( “=” ), then embed that “=” as the defining bars of an F** (or of the *f* in the wordmark) so the mark reads as *Unyform = conformance* at a glance.

---

## Why it fits Unyform (enterprise + edgy)

- **Enterprise-clear meaning:** “=” signals standards, parity, consistency, control.
- **Sleek + sharp:** geometric, minimal, easy to reproduce in product/UI.
- **A little edgy:** the “hidden” U inside the F rewards a second look without becoming gimmicky.
- **Product-true:** Unyform’s identity is about *enforcement* and *governance* at generation-time, not “vibes.” fileciteturn0file0

## Three directions to explore

### Direction A — “Sideways-U equals bars” (icon first)
Build the F from a **strong vertical stem** and a **sideways U** that reads as “=”:

- The sideways U creates **two parallel bars** (the equals sign)
- The U’s curve becomes a subtle **structural corner**
- Result: **F** reads instantly; **U** is discovered

**Best for:** app icon, favicon, VS Code extension tile, docs header.

**Quick sketch (ASCII-ish):**
```
F + sideways-U bars
┃═══
┃═══
┃
```

---

### Direction B — “Double-crossbar *f* in the wordmark”
In *unyform*, customize the **lowercase f**:

- Replace the usual single cross-stroke with a **double bar** ( “=” )
- Shape the terminals to echo the **U** (slight rounding / “U-like” end-caps)
- Optionally echo that motif in the **u** (flat top, squared shoulders, or a subtle notch)

**Best for:** primary wordmark on web/deck; enterprise-friendly because it remains legible and conservative.

**Wordmark cue (not literal):**
- uny**f**orm → the **f** becomes a signature with **=**

---

### Direction C — “UF monogram” (most bold)
Create a compact monogram where **U and F share a stem**:

- Left stem is the “spine”
- Two short bars form “F”
- A bottom curve (or implied curve) completes “U”

**Best for:** brand mark on dark backgrounds, merch, social avatars.

---

## Shape language + constraints (so it stays enterprise)
- **Geometry:** 90° corners, clean straight segments; minimal curvature (only where needed to hint U).
- **Stroke weight:** heavy enough to survive at 16–24px; avoid hairlines.
- **Corner radius:** either *none* (more “sharp”) or a *consistent small radius* (sleek/modern).
- **Avoid looking like:** “F=” as in a school grade joke. Counter this with a distinctive U cue (curved join, negative space U, or wordmark pairing).

---

## Color & finish (pairs well with this mark)
This mark works best in **monochrome** with a single high-signal accent.

- **Base:** near-black / graphite on off-white
- **Accent options:** electric cyan, ultraviolet, acid green (use sparingly for “edgy” moments)
